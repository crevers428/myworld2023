Transportation

Venue

The main competition room is the Plenary at the Melbourne Convention and Exhibition Centre. There are also rooms for side events and relaxing within the venue.
The Plenary is located within the Convention Centre.

Address: 1 Convention Centre Place, South Wharf.
Public Transportation: The Melbourne Convention and Exhibition Centre is located in the heart of Melbourne's South Wharf.

Train The closest train stations are Southern Cross station (9 minute walk), and Flinders Street station (12 minute walk).

Tram on Clarendon Tram routes 96, 109 and 12 (Stop 124A Casino/MCEC) will all take you to the Clarendon Street entrance of the centre.

Tram on FlindersTram routes 48 and 70 (Stop D5) take you to Flinders Street, a short walk away from the venue.


Airport Connection

Melbourne Airport is located approximately 25 kilometres North-West of the city. The cheapest way to travel to the city is via the SkyBus, which departs frequently all 24 hours of the day. A one-way ticket costs 19.50 AUD. The SkyBus runs from the airport to Southern Cross railway station, which is only a 10 minute walk from the competition venue and surrounding areas.

Taxi and Uber

If you’re arriving with a group of three or more people, it may be more cost-effective to take a taxi or Uber. A ride from the airport to the city normally costs between 50 AUD and 65 AUD depending on the time of day.


General Travel Information

  • Public transport within Melbourne is run by PTV. Travel on trains, trams, and buses in the city require the use of a myki card.
  • There is also an extensive free tram zone within the Melbourne CBD, which does not require a myki card.
  • The Melbourne Convention and Exhibition Centre tram stop (Stop 124A Casino/MCEC) is located one stop outside this free zone from the direction of the city.

Visa Information
